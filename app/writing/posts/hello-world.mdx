I'm not much of a blogger, but we all start from somewhere right! This is my first of hopefully more-than-one blog posts.
I have high hopes for this new endeavor, as you may notice from the 5 separate instances of the word "hopefully."
If anyone learns something from this about web development or about me, I'll consider it a smashing success.

## Why I'm Writing

For a long time, I've been building things quietly — side projects, experiments, half-finished ideas that taught me more than the polished ones ever did.
I realized that most of my learnings didn't come from courses or tutorials, it came from tinkering, breaking things, fixing them, and repeating that cycle more times than I can count.
At some point, I thought "maybe I should document this," so here we are.
Not because I have all the answers (P.S: I don't), but because writing forces clarity. And if something I build helps even one person, that's reason enough to hit "publish."

## My Thoughts on Software (The Hill I'll Die On)

These aren't rules. Just principles I try (and sometimes fail) to follow:

1. **<u>Build First, Optimize Later</u>**: Getting something working beats getting something perfect. Clarity comes from iteration, not overthinking.

2. **<u>Simplicity Wins</u>**: If I can remove code and everything still works — that's progress.

3. **<u>Learn in Public</u>**: You don't need to be an expert to share what you know. You just need to be one step ahead of someone else.

4. **<u>Tools Should Serve You</u>**: Whether it's React, Next.js, or a text editor setup — the tool should reduce friction, not become the project.

5. **<u>Read the Docs</u>**: Most confusion disappears when you actually read the documentation. It's underrated. It's unglamorous. It works.

6. **<u>Make It Work. Then Make It Right. Then Make It Fast.</u>**: In that order. Speed without correctness is noise.

7. **<u>Consistency > Cleverness</u>**: I'd rather write boring, predictable code than something "smart" that future-me has to reverse engineer.

8. **<u>Debugging Is a Skill</u>**: Staring at a bug is not debugging. Forming hypotheses is. Logs are your friends.

9. **<u>Small Projects Compound</u>**: Tiny tools, experiments, weekend builds — they stack up. Each one teaches something that textbooks won't.

10. **<u>Shipping Teaches More Than Planning</u>**: You can think about architecture for weeks. Or you can ship something imperfect and learn in a day.

## What's Next?

From here on out, I'll be sharing the projects I'm working on, the bugs that kept me up until 3 AM, and the occasional deep dive into the tech I'm using.

Thanks for stopping by. Let's see where this goes.
